Best Wireless Bras: 11 Options for All Day Comfort

You probably have a few bras in your wardrobe rotation—strapless, push-up, plunge, etc. Notice I didn’t say wireless bras—that’s because it can be challenging to find quality ones that actually provide the necessary support, especially for folks with larger busts. Contrary to popular belief, there are wireless bras that can be worn on a variety of occasions for individuals with both large and small busts. To help ease the process, we consulted with bra experts to learn more about the best bras on the market and how to go about finding the right size and fit.

What to consider when choosing wireless bras

Support

A stereotype about wireless bras is that they don’t offer support, especially to those individuals with larger busts—but that’s not always the case. There are always a few bad apples, but with the right construction, a wireless bra can be just as supportive as an underwire one. “Always opt for adjustable straps or bands if you want something with a better fit for your body,” says Kimmay Caldwell, bra fit trainer and founder of The Fitters' Formula™. Other important qualities to look for include the cup sizes and pads to make sure it’s a good fit for your body.

Coverage

Different types of coverage can be provided by a wireless bra. For example, you may want something that’s more full coverage with thick pads and larger cups. On the other hand, there’s also the opportunity to show more skin with a plunge neckline or push-up bra. You’ll want to consider the coverage that a wireless bra can give you.

Size

Many individuals don’t know their actual bra size and that can be detrimental in the case of looking for a wireless bra. “Expert bra fitters can help determine the best size as well as the best style of wireless bra for your body,” says Mila Stojanovic, bra fit expert at Bravissimo.

Who can wear a wireless bra?

Caldwell says that almost everyone can wear a wireless bra. That said, there are some individuals who may benefit more from wearing one—pregnant people, full figured individuals, elderly folks, and teenagers may all find that wireless bras are more comfortable and well suited to their needs and lifestyles.

How do you measure for a wireless bra?

Our bra experts advise against self-measuring to figure out your bra size. “I would recommend getting a professional fitting, especially if you haven’t had a bra fitting for over six months, as our boobs change more often than you think,” says Stojanovic. “The size should be very similar or the same to your underwired bra size—but a professional bra fitter can really help you narrow down what works best.”

How should a wireless bra fit?

The fit of a wireless bra depends on the type of bra and the brand as each differs slightly. “Wireless bras can be supportive and give the wearer lift. However there’s a higher chance that boobs will be less separated and the shape may feel a touch more flat than when wearing an underwired bra,” says Stojanovic.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Do wireless bras really support?

According to Caldwell, many wireless bras these days can still deliver a great deal of support. As with any type of undergarments, you’ll want to make sure you have the correct size and try a few different types to see which works best for you.

What are the disadvantages of wireless bras?

The disadvantages of wireless bras vary, but Stojanovic says most of the disadvantages can stem in the overall fit, i.e. making boobs appear flatter and less separated. This isn’t necessarily a disadvantage, but something you’ll want to note.

What is the best bra to wear with anything?

Wireless bras are great to wear with anything because they’re usually comfortable, supportive, and provide coverage. That said, if you have an outfit like a dress or tank top, you might want to opt for a strapless bra.

Is it okay to put a wireless bra in the dryer?

Some wireless bras are machine washable, however, there are many that are not. You’ll want to check with the specific care instructions for the bra you have prior to drying it.

Why does my bra roll up under my breasts?

Generally, a bra rolls up under your breasts when you aren’t wearing the right size. “You still want your bust to fit into the cup,” says Caldwell. “If there is an underwire channel or a clear indication of the cup shape, use that as guidance.”

As mentioned above, you can’t go wrong with consulting a bra fit expert to determine your right size.
